Meet the Team

The ILMRL is a diverse group of researchers dedicated to solving complex agricultural challenges through microbiome science.

Principal Investigator

Dr. Hooman Derakhshani

Principal Investigator & Lab Director

Bio: Dr. Derakhshani leads the ILMRL with a focus on decoding the complex interactions between livestock hosts and their resident microbiota. With a background in veterinary medicine and microbial ecology, he drives the lab's strategic direction.

Research Focus:

  • Host-microbe interactions in ruminants and swine.
  • Development of microbiome-based therapeutics.
  • Integration of multi-omics data (metagenomics, metabolomics).

Postdoctoral Fellows

Dr. A S M Zisanur Rahman

Bio: Dr. Rahman is a Postdoctoral Fellow specializing in advanced bioinformatics and microbial genomics. He leads the lab's computational pipelines for analyzing complex metagenomic datasets.

Research Focus: Application of machine learning in microbiome data analysis, rumen methanogen profiling, and resistome analysis in livestock environments.

Graduate Students

PhD Candidate

Nahidur Rahman

Bio: Nahidur is a PhD student investigating the functional dynamics of the bovine microbiome. His work aims to link specific microbial taxa to feed efficiency traits.

Research Focus: Rumen fermentation pathways, mitigation of enteric methane emissions, and long-read sequencing technologies.

MSc Students

Larissa Scott

Bio: Larissa is an MSc student focused on early-life interventions in swine. She is interested in how maternal diet influences piglet gut colonization.

Research Focus: Swine gut health, prebiotics, and immune system development.

Fatemeh Mohammadian

Bio: Fatemeh joined the lab to study the impact of antimicrobials on the livestock microbiome. She brings a strong background in molecular biology.

Research Focus: Antimicrobial resistance (AMR) genes, plasmid transfer in gut communities.

Anindita Roy

Bio: Anindita is researching the respiratory microbiome of cattle. Her work seeks to identify biomarkers for Bovine Respiratory Disease (BRD).

Research Focus: Nasopharyngeal microbiota, respiratory health, and pathogen exclusion.
